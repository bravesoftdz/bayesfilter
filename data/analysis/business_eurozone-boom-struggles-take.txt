Surveys of the private sector indicate that manufacturers have healthy order
books and plenty of business on their hands, but official figures do not show
Industrial production fell by 0.3pc in February compared with January and is up
by just 1.2pc on the year, according to data agency Eurostat.
January’s data was revised at the same time to show output only rose by 0.3pc
The purchasing managers’ index (PMI) for the eurozone is at a six-year high,
and a new survey underlined the strong outlook: the ZEW index of sentiment,
which surveys German investors, jumped sharply from 12.8 in March to 19.5 in
The index aims to predict the state of the economy in the coming six months,
But it does not explain the lack of growth in actual output.
Economists hope that this could be related to the weather and, although
industrial production will dent first quarter economic growth, other sectors
should be accelerating, which will support the overall GDP numbers.
