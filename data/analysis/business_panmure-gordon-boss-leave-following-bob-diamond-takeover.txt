Panmure Gordon boss to leave following Bob Diamond takeover 
Former Barclays CEO Bob Diamond Former Barclays CEO Bob Diamond Credit: ANDY
Panmure Gordon's chief executive Patric Johnson will be replaced by a former
Barclays executive once the takeover of the broker by Bob Diamond goes ahead.  
Mr Johnson, who said weeks ago that he was not stepping down from the role,
will leave Panmure once it is taken over by an investment firm controlled by
the former Barclays boss and a Qatari investment bank in June, the broker said
He will be replaced by Ian Axe, the former chief executive of clearing house
LCH Clearnet who held a number of senior roles at Barclays - including head of
operations for Barclays Capital and Barclays Wealth, global head of equity
finance, and chief operating officer of the investment bank in Europe. He left
in 2011, a year before Mr Diamond resigned from Barclays amid the Libor

executive change, adding he had not given much thought over what he will do
next. "If anything [this] is a compliment to what we've done so far. [He's] the
Mr Johnson took on the top job in February 2016, after former chief executive
Philip Wale departed and chairman Ed Warner resigned, and has since helped
steer the business back to a profit following huge losses in 2015.
