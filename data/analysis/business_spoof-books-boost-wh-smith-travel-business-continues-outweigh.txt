Spoof books boost WH Smith as travel business continues to outweigh high street

The ongoing interest in spoof humour books and new airport shop openings
have driven WH Smith's half-year profits 4pc higher.
In its results for the six months to February 28, the retailer said pre-tax
profits had climbed to £83m, up from £80m in the same period last year.
Stephen Clarke, chief executive, hailed it as a "great result" as it beat last
year's profit upgrade, when its high street shops were turbocharged by the
WH Smith increased its interim dividend by 9pc to 14.6p for the 11th
consecutive year on top of the £50m buyback it announced in October.
