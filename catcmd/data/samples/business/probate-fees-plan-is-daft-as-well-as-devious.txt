This plan is daft as well as devious. Probate fees are not, as you put it,
“payable when claiming inheritances”. They are paid by executors, and in the
case of intestacy, administrators, to obtain authority to administer the estate
of a deceased person. Executors may or may not be beneficiaries. Although the
draft legislation is adamant that this would be an upfront fee, it is only once
probate has been granted that it is possible to assess the value of an estate
taking into account all assets and liabilities. This charge therefore could
only be imposed retrospectively. That would reveal it for what it is: an
increase in inheritance tax, but one not levied in a smoothly progressive
