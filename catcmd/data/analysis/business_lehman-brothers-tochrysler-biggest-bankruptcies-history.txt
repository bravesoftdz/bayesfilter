The collapse of Lehman Brothers in 2008 was the largest and most high profile
casualty of the global credit crisis, and its demise is often seen as the
moment when the entire financial system nearly came crashing down.
When the investment bank filed for bankruptcy, it had around $639bn in assets
and around $619bn in liabilities. The firm was thought to be one of the biggest
dealers in fixed-interest trading on Wall Street and invested heavily in
securities linked to the US sub-prime mortgage market.
